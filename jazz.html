Jazz Improvisation Lessons

Learn to speak the language of jazz — on any instrument.

What we cover

  • Jazz theory: modes, scales and chord-scale relationships
  • Improvising over common chord progressions (blues, rhythm changes, standards)
  • Transcription and learning from the masters
  • Developing your ear — hearing and playing what you hear
  • Swing feel, phrasing and articulation
  • Comping and accompaniment
  • Putting it all together in a real playing context

Any instrument welcome

Jazz improvisation lessons are open to players of any instrument. You do not need to be a trumpet player — if you can already play your instrument to a basic level and want to learn to improvise, we can work together.

Where do I start?

You do not need years of jazz experience. If you can hold a tune on your instrument and have some basic music theory, that is enough to begin. We start with the fundamentals and build from there at your pace.
