# Content guide — what to write, where

Each section below matches a spot on the site. The current text is placeholder — replace it
in your own voice. Aim for warm, plain-spoken and specific; short sentences beat clever ones.
Things that build trust with parents and adult learners: real names, real places you've
played/taught, years of experience, and being clear about practical stuff (price, location,
lesson length, DBS checks).

## Homepage (index.html)

**Hero headline** (the big line at the top)
> Prompt: One sentence that says what you offer and where, in a friendly way. Include
> "Stockport" — it helps Google and tells visitors instantly they're in the right place.
> e.g. the shape of "Friendly music lessons in Stockport, taught by working musicians."

**Hero paragraph** (1–2 sentences under the headline)
> Prompt: Who lessons are for (kids/adults, beginners/returners) and the one thing that
> makes you different from an agency — you're taught directly by the two of us, no middleman.

**Lesson cards** (one line each for Drums / Brass / Jazz / Cello)
> Prompt: One sentence per instrument: what a student will actually get out of lessons.
> Write it about the student ("you'll learn...") not about the subject.

**Meet your teachers** (the two cards with photo circles)
> Prompt for each bio, 2–4 sentences:
> 1. First name + instrument(s).
> 2. One line of credibility — where you studied, bands/orchestras you've played with,
>    how long you've been teaching.
> 3. One line about what lessons with you feel like (patient? fun? structured?).
> 4. Optional human touch — something a parent would smile at.
> Swap the placeholder circle for a photo: replace the contents of the
> `.photo-placeholder` div with `<img src="photos/will.jpg" alt="Will">`.
> Best photos: warm light, smiling, ideally holding/at your instrument. Square crop works best.

## Instrument pages (drums / brass / jazz / cello / piano)

Each page has the same skeleton:

**Page intro** (line under the page title)
> Prompt: One sentence: instrument + who it's for + "Stockport".

**"What we cover" list**
> Prompt: Keep 5–7 bullets. Lead with the things beginners worry about (technique,
> reading), end with the exciting stuff (playing real music, exams if wanted).
> Mention specific exam boards you actually prepare students for.

**Two short blocks** (currently "Who are lessons for?" / "Lesson format" etc.)
> Prompt block 1: Reassure the nervous beginner — no equipment needed to start?
> what age can kids start? adults welcome?
> Prompt block 2: The practical bit — lesson lengths, where lessons happen
> (your home / their home / online?), and roughly what it costs if you're happy to say.

**Teacher strip** (small photo + text above the Book button)
> Prompt: 1–2 sentences connecting *this instrument* to *you specifically*.
> Same photo swap as above.

## Contact page

**Intro line**
> Prompt: Lower the stakes — "no obligation", "happy to answer questions",
> "free taster lesson" if you offer one (a taster offer converts really well).

**Other ways to reach us**
> Replace `your@email.com` (it appears twice — in the form's `action` and in the text).
> Note: the `mailto:` form only opens the visitor's email app, which many people don't
> have set up. When you're ready, swap it for a free form service like Formspree or
> just list email + phone/WhatsApp — parents love WhatsApp.

## Worth adding later
- Testimonials/reviews section on the homepage — even 2 or 3 quotes from current students.
- Prices, if you're comfortable publishing them (filters out time-wasters).
- A line about DBS checks if you teach children.
- Photos of teaching spaces or performances, not just headshots.
